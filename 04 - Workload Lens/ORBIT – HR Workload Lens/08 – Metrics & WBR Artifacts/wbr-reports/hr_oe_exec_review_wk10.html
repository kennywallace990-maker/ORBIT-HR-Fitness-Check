Q1: What Is Causing the Week-to-Week Variance?

The short answer: Three things move the needle

Week-to-week ticket variance is driven by a combination of event-driven spikes, seasonal process artifacts, and phone-channel call volume fluctuations. Automation-generated tickets are remarkably stable and are not a source of variance.

Q3: What Actions Can We Take to Stabilize the Trend?

The variance analysis reveals that week-to-week ticket instability has three addressable root causes. Each has a specific stabilization lever.

#Variance SourceImpactStabilization ActionExpected Effect
1 Phone-channel Attendance Inquiry -110 of -244 total (45% of all variance) Centralized call-off line + UKG TM app. Phone volume is the single largest variance driver because TMs call in absences ad hoc, creating unpredictable daily load. A centralized intake channel would convert unstructured phone calls into structured, trackable submissions with consistent daily volume. Reduces phone volatility and creates a predictable daily intake pattern. Based on the 582→692 range, stabilization target is ~600 ± 30/week.
2 Seasonal process artifacts (I-9/Compliance, Swag) -56 (I-9) and -23 (Swag) = -79 of -244 (32% of variance) Reclassify or re-route. I-9 document submissions and Smartsheet swag automation should not be in FC General Inquiry. Creating dedicated SNOW categories (e.g., "Compliance Document Submission," "Swag Request") removes seasonal noise from the inquiry trend line. This is a SNOW configuration change, not a process change. Removes ~55-110 predictable but noisy tickets/week from FC General Inquiry. Remaining FC volume becomes a cleaner demand signal. Seasonal spikes become visible in their own category instead of distorting the inquiry trend.
3 UTO balance confusion (structural, cross-channel) ~700 Attendance + ~200 CC T&A = ~900/week (46% of total volume) UKG TM app — PTO/UTO balance visibility. UTO-related inquiries are the structural floor of Attendance Inquiry volume. They don't cause variance per se (they're consistently high), but they create a high baseline from which any spike looks alarming. Reducing the baseline through self-service makes the remaining variance smaller in absolute terms and easier to explain. A 20% reduction in UTO inquiries would remove ~180 tickets/week from the baseline, making the remaining ±100/week variance represent ~10% instead of ~5.5% of total — a psychologically more stable trend for OBR reporting.
Immediate OBR improvement — separate automation from manual. Your xlsx analysis already identified this: adding an "Automation vs Manual" split to the OBR would make weekly variance immediately explainable. Automation held at exactly 205 Attendance Inquiry + 11 FC General Inquiry = 216 tickets. Manual dropped from 1,369 to 1,101 (-268). Reporting these separately eliminates the noise floor and focuses attention on actionable human-initiated demand.

Recommended OBR Reporting Changes

  1. Split automation vs manual in the weekly volume table. Show automation as a separate stable line.
  2. Add contact type breakdown for Attendance Inquiry (Phone / Email / Self Service). Phone is the variance driver.
  3. Flag seasonal categories (I-9/Compliance, Swag) separately from true inquiries in FC General Inquiry.
  4. Include "Attendance Management" (438 tickets) in next analysis — EPA tracks it as a separate service. These are automated NCNS/UTO-negative-balance notifications that provide context for Attendance Inquiry volume.
  5. Track Monday volume as a leading indicator. Monday accounts for ~20% of weekly volume and sets the tone for the rest of the week.

Data Caveats & Methodology

  • Scope: 4 of ~115 EPA HR Service categories. Our 1,962 tickets represent 26.1% of EPA's 7,525 total Week 10 volume.
  • Excluded per request: Leave of Absence (Status + Action Needed), SLA adherence analysis.
  • CS = CC: EPA OBR uses "CS Time and Attendance." We use "CC Time and Attendance." Same service — Contact Center was previously named Customer Service.
  • Week boundaries: Sunday through Saturday. Week 9 = 2/22-2/28. Week 10 = 3/1-3/7.
  • Classification: Keyword-based, first-match-wins, deterministic. Accuracy ~90-93% based on prior manual review.
  • Resolution rate caveat: Week 10 rates appear lower because the extract was pulled shortly after week-end, leaving late-opened tickets unresolved. This is the same timing artifact observed in prior weeks.
  • Automation detection: Tickets containing "smartsheet" or "automation@app" in Description1 are classified as automation-generated.
  • Site extraction: Parsed from Assignment Group. Centralized teams (WFM, RTA, TMDM, TMSC, LOA/ADA) are grouped as "CENTRALIZED."
  • UKG Phase I data: Not included in this analysis — will be integrated once provided.
