Section 4: Historical Corrections (Retro-Pay Risk)

What is a Historical Correction? An HR-initiated change to a payroll record from a previous pay period that has already been transmitted. These are the most costly type of rework — they require retroactive payroll entries and often signal a process or policy breakdown upstream.

There were 1,875 Historical Corrections this week, representing HR retroactively touching closed pay periods.

  • Attendance Enforcement (671 actions): Indicating local managers or HR failed to code lates/absences prior to the payroll active window closing. This is the #1 retro-pay driver this week.
  • Core Pay & Missing Time (358 actions): High Friction — indicating employees required retroactive payroll deposits for missed regular, premium, or overtime pay.
  • Leave & Compliance Lag (351 actions): Standard expected lag pending documentation (FMLA, Accommodations). Typically the hardest to reduce due to external approval dependencies.
  • Schedule & Unpaid True-Ups (328 actions): Indicating broad operational changes (VTO, Weather) were not processed prior to transmission.
  • Other (167 actions): Various other discrepancies requiring retroactive correction.
AI Insight — Historical Corrections

Attendance Enforcement is the leading cause of retroactive payroll changes (671 of 1,875 = 35.8%). These are preventable — late arrival and call-off codes should be entered during the active pay period, not after close. The 358 Core Pay corrections are the highest-friction items (each scored at 5 friction points), representing cases where employees didn't receive correct pay on time. Combined, these two categories consumed an estimated 85.8 FTE hours of retroactive processing.

Appendix B: Glossary of Metric Definitions

1. Full-Time Equivalent (FTE) Hours
Translates raw click volume into real labor cost. 40 FTE Hours = one full-time employee working an entire week doing nothing but manual data entry.
2. Friction Time Cost
Every UKG action is weighted by complexity: Historical Corrections = 5 pts (~5 min), Standard Edits/Deletes = 1 pt (~1 min), Governance = 0.5 pts (~30 sec). Total points ÷ 60 = Friction Hours.
3. Defect Rate
% of total HR workload dedicated to fixing mistakes. Any correction, edit, or delete is a defect — the timecard should have been correct the first time. Approvals/reviews (Governance) are not penalized.
4. DPMO (Defects Per Million Opportunities)
Standardized Six Sigma metric. Calculates how many defects would occur if a site had exactly 1,000,000 expected punch opportunities, allowing a 50-person site to be fairly compared against a 5,000-person site.
