Q1: What Is Causing the Week-to-Week Variance in Ticket Volumes?

The Answer: Three Structural Forces

Week-to-week ticket variance is not random. It is driven by three identifiable, measurable forces acting simultaneously:

  1. Phone-channel call volume fluctuations — 45% of all variance
  2. Seasonal process artifacts (I-9 renewals, swag automation) — 32% of variance
  3. Event-driven demand spikes (weather, site-specific anomalies) — 23% of variance

Critically, automation-generated tickets are perfectly stable (205 Attendance Inquiry, 11 FC General Inquiry = 216/week). The entire -244 ticket decline came from human-initiated demand. And UKG data reveals the ticket queue captures only ~8% of actual rework — the variance we see in tickets is the tip of a much larger iceberg.

The Bottom Line for Leadership:
  • The -244 ticket decline does NOT mean HR workload decreased. UKG shows ~67,500 actions/week, consuming 761 FTE hours. Ticket volume swings reflect TM behavior (calling less), not HR capacity changes.
  • The variance is explainable: Phone call fluctuations (45%), seasonal compliance artifacts (32%), and weather/event normalization (23%).
  • UTO confusion is the structural story. It appears in every channel, every week, and is the highest-friction issue per UKG action. Self-service UTO balance visibility is the highest-ROI intervention available.
  • 92% of HR rework is invisible to the OBR. Adding the UKG rework coverage metric to the OBR would fundamentally change how leadership understands HR operational capacity.

Data Caveats & Methodology

  • Ticket scope: 4 of ~115 EPA HR Service categories. Our 1,962 tickets represent 26.1% of EPA's 7,525 Wk10 total.
  • UKG scope: Week 9 data (02/23–03/01). 67,543 actions across 51 sites, 4 BUs. Excludes self-service, Team Member, Other, Automation actor groups.
  • Week boundaries: Sunday through Saturday. Wk9 = 2/22–2/28. Wk10 = 3/1–3/7.
  • Ticket classification: Keyword-based, first-match-wins, deterministic. ~90–93% accuracy per prior manual review.
  • UKG site estimates: Site-level UKG action counts in the site-variance table are directional estimates from Week 9 proportional distribution. Exact Wk10 UKG data pending view deployment.
  • Excluded: Leave of Absence (Status + Action Needed), SLA adherence analysis, Attendance Management (438 tix — requesting CSV).
  • Cross-phase join: Ticket-to-UKG ratios are directional coverage proxies, not 1:1 linkages. No shared identifier exists between SNOW and UKG today.
  • Resolution rate caveat: Wk10 rates appear lower due to extract timing. Same pattern observed in prior weeks.
